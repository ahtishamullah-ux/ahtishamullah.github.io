Ahtisham Ullah

Mechanical Engineer · PLM Specialist · Research Lead

📘 About this page: This is the official professional biography of Ahtisham Ullah, a Pakistani-Italian mechanical engineer based in Lecce, Italy, specializing in PLM systems, Industry 5.0 research, and green hydrogen technologies.

Ahtisham Ullah (born in Gujranwala, Pakistan) is a Pakistani-Italian mechanical engineer, PLM specialist, and research leader based in Lecce, Italy. He currently works at EKA S.R.L as a PLM Specialist managing PTC Windchill PLM systems for diverse international clients, while simultaneously leading advanced research at CORELAB, University of Salento in Industry 5.0 technologies.

Ullah holds a Master of Science in Management Engineering from the University of Salento (101/110) and a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore (CGPA 3.351/4.0). He is currently enrolled in the DII - Territorial Pact for Higher Education - Advanced Training Course in Digital Technologies and Organizational Methodologies for Industry 5.0, funded by Patto Territoriale del Sistema Universitario Pugliese.

He is recognized internationally for his work on green hydrogen production, Product Lifecycle Management (PLM), sustainable industrial processes, and supply chain optimization, with approximately three years of professional experience spanning Pakistan, Italy, and Spain. Ullah is a recipient of the AMAL Academy Career Preparation Fellowship (funded by Stanford University) and a participant in the European Union Erasmus+ Programme.

Biography

Ahtisham Ullah was born and raised in Gujranwala, Pakistan, a major industrial city in the Punjab province. From an early age, he demonstrated a strong aptitude for mathematics, science, and problem-solving, which led him to pursue engineering as his career path.

He completed his matriculation at Government Postgraduate Model High School in 2015 with 90% marks, followed by F.Sc Pre-Engineering at Superior Group of Colleges, Gujranwala in 2017 with 88% marks. His excellent academic performance earned him admission to one of Pakistan's most prestigious engineering institutions.

After earning his bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering, Ullah moved to Italy in 2022 to pursue advanced studies in management engineering, focusing on the intersection of engineering, business management, and sustainable technologies. He has since established himself as a researcher and industry professional in the European engineering community.

Master's Thesis

Ullah's master's thesis, titled "Deployment of Hydrogen Based Energy Solution for Hard-to-Abate Industrial Processes and Sectors", was supervised by Prof. Claudio Petti and Prof. Giuseppina Passiante at the University of Salento. The research focused on techno-economic analysis of hydrogen-based energy systems for industrial decarbonization.

Bachelor's Thesis

His undergraduate thesis, "Design and Development of Indirect Evaporative Cooler (M-Cycle)", involved advanced 2-D Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulation using COMSOL Multiphysics software, demonstrating his strong foundation in thermal engineering and numerical analysis.
