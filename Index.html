Ahtisham Ullah

Mechanical Engineer · PLM Specialist · Industry 5.0 Researcher

📘 About: This is the official professional biography of Ahtisham Ullah, a Pakistani-Italian mechanical engineer based in Lecce, Italy, specializing in Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) systems, Industry 5.0 research, and green hydrogen technologies.

Ahtisham Ullah (born in Gujranwala, Pakistan) is a Pakistani-Italian mechanical engineer, PLM specialist, and research leader based in Lecce, Italy. He currently works at EKA S.R.L as a PLM Specialist managing PTC Windchill Product Lifecycle Management systems for diverse international clients, while simultaneously leading advanced research at CORELAB, University of Salento in Industry 5.0 technologies.

Ullah holds a Master of Science in Management Engineering from the University of Salento (101/110, 2024) and a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore (CGPA 3.351/4.0, 2021). He is currently enrolled in the DII - Territorial Pact for Higher Education - Advanced Training Course in Digital Technologies and Organizational Methodologies for Industry 5.0, funded by Patto Territoriale del Sistema Universitario Pugliese (Territorial Pact of the Apulian University System).

He is recognized internationally for his work on green hydrogen production, Product Lifecycle Management (PLM), digital transformation, and sustainable industrial processes, with approximately three years of professional experience spanning Pakistan, Italy, and Spain. Ullah is a recipient of the AMAL Academy Career Preparation Fellowship (funded by Stanford University, 2019) and a participant in the European Union Erasmus+ Programme (2024).

Biography

Ahtisham Ullah was born and raised in Gujranwala, Pakistan, a major industrial city in the Punjab province known for its manufacturing and engineering industries. From an early age, he demonstrated exceptional aptitude in mathematics, physics, and problem-solving, which naturally led him toward pursuing a career in engineering.

He completed his matriculation (SSC) at Government Postgraduate Model High School, Gujranwala in 2015, achieving 90% marks. He then pursued F.Sc Pre-Engineering at Superior Group of Colleges, Gujranwala, graduating in 2017 with 88% marks. His outstanding academic performance earned him admission to the University of Engineering and Technology (UET), Lahore, one of Pakistan's most prestigious engineering institutions.

During his undergraduate studies, Ullah actively participated in professional societies, serving as General Secretary of the ASHRAE Student Chapter at UET Lahore (2019-2021). He also received the AMAL Academy Career Preparation Fellowship, a highly competitive program funded by Stanford University, which provided him with advanced career development training and leadership skills.

After earning his bachelor's degree in 2021, Ullah moved to Lecce, Italy in 2022 to pursue advanced studies in management engineering at the University of Salento. His research focused on the intersection of engineering, business management, and sustainable technologies, particularly in the field of green hydrogen energy systems. Since then, he has established himself as both a researcher and industry professional in the European engineering community, contributing to cutting-edge projects in PLM systems and Industry 5.0 technologies.

Master's Thesis Research

Ullah's master's thesis, titled "Deployment of Hydrogen Based Energy Solution for Hard-to-Abate Industrial Processes and Sectors", was completed in 2024 under the supervision of Prof. Claudio Petti and Prof. Giuseppina Passiante at the University of Salento. The research provided comprehensive techno-economic analysis of hydrogen-based energy systems for industrial decarbonization, focusing on sectors such as steel production, cement manufacturing, and chemical processing that are difficult to electrify directly.

Bachelor's Thesis Project

His undergraduate thesis, "Design and Development of Indirect Evaporative Cooler (M-Cycle)", was completed in 2021 at UET Lahore. The project involved advanced two-dimensional Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulation using COMSOL Multiphysics software, demonstrating his strong foundation in thermal engineering, heat transfer analysis, and numerical methods.

Ongoing Training

Currently enrolled in the Advanced Training Course in Digital Technologies and Organizational Methodologies to Support Industry 5.0, a prestigious program organized under the DII - Territorial Pact for Higher Education initiative, funded by the Patto Territoriale del Sistema Universitario Pugliese. This advanced training focuses on next-generation manufacturing technologies, human-centric automation, and sustainable industrial transformation.
